# Remediation Loop

The standing protocol I run **whenever you share a worked solution** — to a practice problem, a quiz question, or a problem raised in discussion. Its job is not just to mark right/wrong but to **find the weakness behind a miss and rebuild the mental model deeper than before.** Linked from every problem set, [[Score Log]], and [[Curriculum]]. Weaknesses are tracked in [[Learning Log]].

## Why it exists

A wrong answer is data. In a strict environment (see the rubric in [[Score Log]]) the *reason* you missed matters more than the miss: the same misconception, left unnamed, resurfaces three modules later wearing a different costume. This loop catches it at the source.

## The five steps (run on every miss)

1. **Classify the error.** Every miss is one of four types — I name which:
   - **Conceptual** — the mental model itself is wrong (e.g. "thought multiplying complex numbers adds magnitudes"). *Most important to fix.*
   - **Procedural** — right idea, execution slip (algebra, sign, arithmetic).
   - **Definitional / units** — term used loosely, units dropped or mismatched.
   - **Transfer** — can't connect this to a prior module (e.g. didn't see the ODE characteristic equation *is* the eigenvalue equation).
2. **Name the misconception in one sentence.** Precise and specific — "you treated $j\omega$ as a magnitude instead of a rotation," not "review complex numbers."
3. **Point to the exact section.** The specific concept-page heading to revisit (e.g. [[Sinusoids And Phasors]] → *From wave to phasor*).
4. **Re-explain from a new angle.** Not a repeat of the page — a *fresh* analogy, derivation, or picture aimed straight at the misconception, going one level deeper than the original page. The goal is a stronger model, not a louder one.
5. **Confirm with a twin problem.** A new problem, same concept, different numbers. Passing it closes the loop; missing it escalates (I go deeper still, or drop back a prerequisite).

## Tracking

- Each named weakness is logged in [[Learning Log]] → **Weakness Log** with its error type and the date.
- A weakness is marked **resolved** only when its twin problem is passed cleanly.
- Unresolved weaknesses in a module **hold back mastery** (🌱→🌿) even if the gate quiz was scraped, and they seed the **Review Queue**.

## How practice relates to the gate

- **Problem sets** ([[Problems P0 Math Toolkit]], [[Problems P1 Linear Algebra]], [[Problems P2 Calculus And Differential Equations]]) are **formative** — open-book drills with rising complexity, run through this loop, meant to *build* skill.
- **Quizzes** ([[Quiz P0 Math Toolkit]] etc.) are **summative** — closed-book gates that *certify* it.
- Recommended order per module: **read the concept pages → work the problem set (loop on every miss) → sit the gate quiz.**
